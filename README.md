**Corbel Design Using IS 456:2000**
This repository presents a structured and reproducible framework for the analysis and design of reinforced concrete corbels subjected to concentrated loads. Corbels are short cantilever projections where traditional beam theory is not applicable due to the dominance of shear and compression strut action.

Design Philosophy:
Corbels behave differently from flexural members due to their low shear span-to-depth ratio (a/d < 1). The load is transferred through a compression strut extending from the load point to the support, while reinforcement acts as a tension tie resisting splitting forces.
The design is therefore based on:
-Equilibrium of forces in a strut-and-tie system
-Limiting stresses in concrete struts
-Adequate anchorage of reinforcement to ensure force transfer

The implementation focuses on design procedures based on IS 456, supplemented with strut-and-tie modeling principles for improved clarity in load transfer mechanisms.
